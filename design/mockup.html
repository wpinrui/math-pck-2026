CPA Approach Definition
Concrete–Pictorial–Abstract. A pedagogical sequence: start with hands-on manipulatives, move to visual representations (diagrams, bar models), then to symbolic/algebraic notation.
Pentagon Model — Five Components List

  • Processes — reasoning, communication, modelling, heuristics
  • Metacognition — monitoring thinking, self-regulation
  • Attitudes — beliefs, interest, confidence, perseverance
CPA vs Spiral Approach Comparison
CPA
Sequences the mode of representation — concrete to pictorial to abstract within a single topic
Spiral
Revisits the same topics at increasing depth across grade levels
Zone of Proximal Development Definition
The gap between what a learner can do independently and what they can achieve with scaffolding from a more knowledgeable other. (Vygotsky)
"Multiplication always makes bigger" Misconception
Common Misconception
Students often believe multiplication always increases a number. This fails with fractions less than 1 and with negative numbers.
Multiplication scales by the multiplier. If the multiplier is between 0 and 1, the result is smaller.

Proportional Reasoning Concept
A unifying thread across the curriculum: ratios, percentages, rate, gradient, similar figures, probability, and linear functions all rely on proportional relationships. Teaching these connections explicitly helps students see mathematics as coherent rather than fragmented.

What is the CPA approach in mathematics education?
click to flip
The CPA approach (Concrete–Pictorial–Abstract) sequences mathematical learning through three stages:

Concrete — hands-on manipulatives (e.g. algebra tiles, fraction bars)
Pictorial — visual representations (e.g. diagrams, bar models)
Abstract — symbolic notation (e.g. algebraic expressions)

It is a key pedagogical principle in the Singapore mathematics curriculum.
